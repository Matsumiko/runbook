# Polish Checklist

Use this list as the final audit before calling a frontend surface polished.

## Visual Hierarchy

- Is the primary action obvious?
- Is the information density appropriate?
- Do headings, labels, helper text, and metadata read in the right order?
- Does the eye know where to go first?

## Spacing and Rhythm

- Are paddings and gaps consistent with nearby surfaces?
- Do sections breathe evenly?
- Are alignments intentional and clean?
- Does the layout feel crowded or underfilled on common breakpoints?

## Component Consistency

- Do buttons, cards, inputs, badges, and tables feel like the same product?
- Are border radii, shadows, stroke weights, and typography treatments coherent?
- Are there any one-off styles that should be pulled back to the family?

## State Coverage

- loading
- empty
- error
- disabled
- hover
- focus
- active
- selected

Not every surface needs every state, but relevant states must not be skipped casually.

## Responsive Quality

- Does the surface hold together on mobile and desktop?
- Does wrapping feel intentional?
- Do important controls stay reachable without awkward overflow?
- Does hierarchy survive the smaller layout?

## Ship-Readiness

The surface is only polished when the rough edges are actually removed, not merely restyled.
