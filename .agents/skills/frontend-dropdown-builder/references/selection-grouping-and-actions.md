# Selection Grouping And Actions

Dropdown quality comes from hierarchy clarity.

## Grouping

- Group related actions together when the list is longer than a few rows.
- Separate destructive or account-sensitive actions from routine ones.
- Use dividers and small group labels sparingly and only when they improve scanning.

## Row content

- Keep labels short and specific.
- Use leading icons only when they help recognition and match the product.
- Show current selection clearly with checkmarks, radio markers, or emphasized label state.
- Disabled rows should remain legible enough to explain availability without pretending to be active.

## Mixed behavior caution

Avoid menus that mix too many row types unless the existing product already does so:

- navigation links
- destructive actions
- toggles
- nested submenus
- form inputs

If the menu becomes a control surface rather than a compact menu, move to a popover.
